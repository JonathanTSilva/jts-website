## Hello, welcome.

If you're reading this, the site is live — I posted it somewhere (probably LinkedIn, my only social network at the moment) — and this is the post I'll always point to when someone asks where it all started.

My name is Jonathan. I'm catholic, a husband, a father, an engineer, a lifelong learner, and a business adventurer. I spend most of my days working on Software Engineering applied to the embedded world — Linux and RTOS systems for industrial applications. I lead teams and try to keep the code clean and the systems reliable. Outside of work, I'm usually reading, tinkering with something tech-related (almost certainly something in my Homelab), or writing about what I'm learning.

This site is something I've wanted to do for a while. Not just as a portfolio or a personal branding exercise — but also as a place to write about the things I find interesting and keep a record of what I'm learning. Embedded systems, software engineering, tools, books, ideas. Whatever feels worth sharing.

For a long time I kept my note-taking system — combining Zettelkasten and PARA — in a private Obsidian vault, with tens of thousands of notes. So I built a system that lets me keep writing in my vault while selecting what I want to publish here. There's a blog (you're in it), a notes section for shorter and more informal things, and a now page where I try to track what I'm currently focused on.

I'll write in both English and Portuguese here. Some posts will be in one language only, some in both — it depends on what I'm writing about and who I'm trying to reach. (And also on whether there are enough tokens left for a good translation.)

That's it, really. No grand mission statement, no content strategy. Just my place to write and share ideas.
